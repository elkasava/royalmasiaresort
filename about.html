The Original Sanctuary

A Century of Welcome

Royal Masia was born of a vision. In 1924, Governor Henri de Masia commissioned the construction of a private estate on 14 acres of tropical land — a place for the governor and his guests to retreat from the demands of colonial administration, and to rediscover the art of unhurried living.

The estate opened its doors to the public in 1938, quickly becoming the address of choice for dignitaries, writers, artists, and adventurers passing through the tropics. Winston Churchill reputedly wrote three chapters of a memoir in our garden library. Ava Gardner stayed for six weeks, calling it "the only place in the tropics that feels like home."

Today, 100 years on, Royal Masia remains privately owned and independently managed — a rarity in the modern luxury landscape. The 14-acre estate, the century-old trees, and the philosophy of genuine, unhurried hospitality are unchanged. Only the thread count and the cocktail list have improved.

A History in Moments

One Hundred Years of Masia

1924

The Estate is Founded

Governor Henri de Masia commissions the construction of a private colonial estate on 14 acres of tropical land. Architect Pierre Lafont designs the main building's iconic colonnaded verandah, which survives intact to this day.

1938

The Doors Open

Following the Governor's death, his family opens Royal Masia to paying guests. The estate quickly becomes the most coveted address in the tropics, hosting politicians, artists, and explorers from across the world.

1952

The Colonial Bar is Born

Renowned bartender Luciano Ferreira joins the Royal Masia team and creates the Long Masia Sling — a cocktail that becomes the resort's most enduring signature, still served unchanged from the original 1952 recipe.

1978

The Gardens Are Established

Botanist and garden designer Eleanor Ashworth-Young spends five years establishing Royal Masia's celebrated estate gardens, introducing rare orchid varieties and the kitchen herb garden that still supplies our restaurants today.

2001

The Masia Spa Opens

Following a two-year restoration of the east wing, the Masia Spa opens — blending ancestral healing traditions with a contemporary sensibility. Within its first year, it is recognised as one of the finest spa experiences in the region.

2024

A Centenary Celebrated

Royal Masia marks its centenary with the completion of the Royal Villa — a standalone private residence within the estate — and the publication of "One Hundred Years of Masia," a limited-edition history of the property available to guests upon request.
